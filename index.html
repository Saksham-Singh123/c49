Origami Bird



These are the steps for a origami pigeon bird:

  • Start with a square piece of origami paper
  • Fold the paper in half by folding the top corner to the bottom corner. You should have an upside down triangle
  • Fold part of the top down so that the edge is about halfway down. Don't make this flap too thin because this will become the wings.
  • Turn the paper over.
  • Take the bottom corner of the top layer and fold it up
  • Fold the figure in half by folding the left side over to the right.
  • : Now fold that same flap back like this to make one of the wings.
  • Fold the other wing back also.
  • Push the center of the head in and flatten. This is called an inside reverse fold.
And tada! You have yourself a bird. Now set it free! Just kidding. Don't throw it in the air. It will just fall down and you'll be disappointed.

Origami Boston Terrier



Do you own or know someone who owns a boston terrier? Surprise them or uh...yourself with this simple and cute looking origami boston terrier.
Steps are as follows:
  • Start with a square piece of origami paper
  • Fold the paper in half by folding the top corner to the bottom corner.
  • Fold the bottom corner of the top layer up like this.
  • Turn the paper over
  • Again, fold the bottom corner up like this.
  • Mentally divide the paper into 3 parts and fold the left 1/3rd to the right..
  • Fold the right 1/3rd over to the left.
  • Fold those same flaps up diagonally.
  • Turn the paper over.
  • Fold back the top left and right corners of the square to make its head a little rounder.

Flapping Butterfly


This brilliantly designed origami flapping butterfly by deg farrelly is fun to play with and fortunately, easy to make.

The steps are as follows:
  • Start with a square sheet of origami paper, color side down.
  • Simply fold the bottom half to the top half, making sure the edges meet, then unfold.
  • Fold the top and bottom edges to meet at the center.
  • Fold in all four corners so their edges meet at the center horizontal crease.
  • Fold down the top half.
  • Fold the center of the bottom half to the top half. Crease about one inch (2.5 cm) of the center only with your fingernail. Do not crease all the way. Remove your finger and there should be a crease in the center only.
  • Turn the paper over.
  • Place a finger on top of the center of the paper and with your other hand, wrap the paper snugly around your finger.
  • Remove your finger and you should have a loop like this.
  • Use a fingernail and poke in the center of the bottom of the loop.
  • With your forefinger and thumb, grab both ends of the loop holes and pinch them together. Pinch and release to make the butterfly flap!
